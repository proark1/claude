Chapter 1 · 60 seconds, zero jargon

What is Claude Code?

Imagine a skilled builder who lives inside your computer. You describe what you want in normal language — the builder does all the technical work, shows you the result, and asks your permission before doing anything important. That's Claude Code. It's an app, like any other app, with buttons and panels. No black screens with green text.

🛡️

You can't break your computer

This fear stops more beginners than anything else, so let's kill it now: Claude Code works inside one project folder, asks before important actions, and every change can be rewound. Worst case? You say “undo that.”

↩️

Everything can be undone

The app automatically saves a restore point every time you send a message. Later we'll show you the exact two-key shortcut that opens the rewind menu.

👀

You stay in charge

Claude proposes, you decide. Files it creates are normal files in a normal folder on your computer — you can look at them anytime, even if you can't read them yet.

Chapter 3 · your supporting cast

Two free accounts that make you unstoppable.

Create these now; Claude Code will handle the technical connections later, when we ask it to. You'll never touch the complicated parts.

🐙

GitHub — the safe home for your project

A on GitHub is where your project lives safely: every version remembered, nothing ever lost, even if your laptop takes a swim. When we're ready, you'll simply tell Claude “save this to my GitHub” — it does the pushing and pulling for you.

Sign up free: go to github.com/signup → enter your email, choose a password and a username → confirm the verification code GitHub emails you. Done.

🚄

Railway — the service that puts you online

Railway takes your project and puts it on the real internet — this is called . It can also give your future projects a — that's a Builder-route adventure.

Sign up: go to railway.com and choose “Sign in with GitHub” — one click, using the account you just made. This also links the two services together, which we'll need in chapter 7.

💰Honest money note: GitHub is free, full stop. Railway gives new accounts a one-time $5 trial credit (lasts up to 30 days) — plenty for this tutorial. Brand-new GitHub accounts sometimes get a “limited” trial; if Railway asks, you can verify your account at railway.com/verify. After the trial, keeping a site up may need the Hobby plan ($5/month). If you'd rather pay nothing ever, chapter 7 has a one-line free alternative too.

The interview trick

Instead of guessing what to write, ask Claude to ask you. It pulls the idea out of your head one friendly question at a time, then turns your answers into a professional build plan. Steal this opener word for word:

Copy this prompt I want a personal website. Ask me questions one at a time about what it should include and how it should look. Then write me a complete build plan in plain language.

The anatomy of a great prompt

Build me a one-page personal website for friends, family and future clients to find me. It must include: my name and photo, a short bio, a photo gallery, and a contact button. Look & feel: warm colors, big friendly type, works beautifully on phones. Ask me questions if anything is unclear.

The golden loop 🔁

Look → say what you want different → look again. You never touch the code. “Make the header bigger.” “Try something warmer.” “Add a section for my photos.” Short, honest, human sentences are the entire skill.

Those permission cards, decoded

When Claude wants to create files or run something, the app pauses and asks. That's a feature, not a problem — you're the boss signing off. Next to the send button you'll find the mode selector:

ManualAsks before each important action. Your home for this whole tutorial.
Accept editsAuto-approves file edits, still asks for bigger things. For when you trust the flow.
PlanClaude only proposes a plan — touches nothing until you approve. Great for big ideas.
…and moreOther modes exist for pros. The Builder route tours them properly.

Chapter 6 · quality time

Test it like a pro (without being technical).

Claude Code has a built-in Browser pane — a little browser inside the app that opens your site automatically as Claude works. Claude even checks its own work in there. But you are the taste-tester. Click every button. Read every word. Then use the superpower below.

The superpower: screenshots are instructions 📸

Most beginners never learn this: you can paste a screenshot straight into the message box (with the + button, drag & drop, or Ctrl/Cmd + V) and say “this looks wrong.” Claude sees the image. Watch it happen →

1 · What you did“I clicked the contact button…”
2 · What you expected“…expecting my email app to open…”
3 · What happened“…but nothing happened at all.”

Those three sentences are better bug reports than most professionals write. Truly.

Chapter 7 · the payoff

Put it on the internet. For real.

Two moves: first your project gets its safe home on GitHub (Claude does it), then Railway puts it online (a few clicks on their website). Fifteen minutes from now, your site has an address.

Move 1 — Claude saves it to GitHub

One sentence. Claude creates the and your files for you. It may ask you to confirm a GitHub sign-in — that's normal, click through it.

Move 2 — Railway puts it online

This part happens on Railway's website, in your normal browser. Because your site is a simple one, Railway needs zero configuration — it recognizes it automatically.

Go to railway.com/new

Sign in (with GitHub — you linked them in chapter 3) and choose “Deploy from GitHub repo.”

Pick my-website

Railway may ask permission to see your GitHub repositories the first time — allow it, then select your project. It starts building immediately.

Wait for the green “Success”

Usually under a minute. Railway automatically detects your site and serves it. From now on, every time Claude pushes an update to GitHub, Railway re-publishes it. Automatically. Forever.

Generate your address

Click your service, then Settings → Networking → Generate Domain. Railway hands you a free address ending in .up.railway.app — that's your site's home on the internet.

🧭Stuck anywhere? Ask Claude Code — really. Paste a screenshot of the Railway screen you're looking at and ask “what do I click?” It knows the way. Chapter 8 · going forward

The five habits of people who build.

1

Talk like a smart colleague

Context and plain language beat technical vocabulary every time. Say why you want something and Claude makes better choices.

2

Small steps, then look

Ask, check, ask again. Ten small requests beat one giant one — you catch wrong turns while they're still cheap.

3

When in doubt, ask Claude to explain

“Explain what you just did like I'm new to this” is always allowed. You'll be surprised how fast you start understanding.

4

Nothing is ever ruined

Checkpoints rewind mistakes. GitHub remembers every version. Fear of breaking things is the only real obstacle — and you've deleted it.

5

Save to GitHub when you're happy

End every good session with “save this to GitHub.” It's the “save game” button for your project — and it re-publishes your live site too.

★

Bonus: keep a wish list

Keep a note of ideas (“add a dark photo page”, “make a site for mom”). Each one is a fun evening with Claude now. That's your new reality.
